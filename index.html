THESIS PROJECT

ReColor — Mobile Vision Assistive App

Real-time color vision remapping for color blind users. Daltonization algorithms, CIE Lab color space — clinically validated with PERI. Submitted to Google Play Store.

BEST CS THESIS
BEST RESEARCH
ABSTRACT

Color vision deficiency (CVD) affects approximately 350 million individuals worldwide, including an estimated 5.17% of Filipino males, predominantly with the deutan subtype. Despite its prevalence, existing mobile assistive tools are often limited to single-function applications that do not integrate screening, correction, and awareness. This study presents ReColor, an Android-based mobile vision assistive application designed to address this gap through a unified set of image processing techniques.

The system integrates four core components: LMS-based daltonization for color enhancement, CIELAB ΔE-based color identification, GPU-accelerated CVD simulation, and HSV-based hue rotation as an alternative enhancement method. A digitized Ishihara screening module is also incorporated to support preliminary detection. Additionally, a U-Net convolutional neural network was developed and evaluated as a research extension, but was not deployed in the final system due to computational constraints.

A mixed-methods evaluation was conducted with 30 general end users. Results indicate that daltonization and hue rotation provide complementary performance across different CVD types, while the color identification module achieved an accuracy of 100%. GPU-based simulation demonstrated high fidelity, with a mean ΔE of 0.025 compared to a double-precision CPU reference. System testing achieved a 100% pass rate across defined test cases, and user evaluation yielded a grand mean of 3.60 (Highly Acceptable) with strong internal consistency (Cronbach’s α = 0.9348).

The study was conducted under a Memorandum of Understanding with the Philippine Eye Research Institute (PERI), ensuring institutional support and alignment with national vision health initiatives under Republic Act No. 11358. By integrating screening, assistive enhancement, and awareness features into a single mobile platform, ReColor provides a practical, accessible, and scalable solution for supporting individuals with color vision deficiency and advancing inclusive technology in the Philippine context.
