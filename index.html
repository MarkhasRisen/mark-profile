CODE_000 ReColor — Thesis Project AI · Computer Vision · Color Accessibility OPEN

ReColor: Mobile Vision Assistive App for Color Vision Deficiency

BEST CS THESIS
BEST RESEARCH · LPU

Production-grade mobile application for real-time color vision remapping, published on the Google Play Store. Built for the estimated 8% of the global male population affected by color vision deficiency (CVD). Applies Daltonization algorithms, K-Means clustering, and Lab color space transformations on the device camera feed — enabling users with Protanopia, Deuteranopia, and Tritanopia to perceive color-differentiated content accurately. Clinically validated with the Philippine Eye Research Institute (PERI).

Architecture

Modular React Native app with 4 dedicated image processing services — separation of concerns across data ingestion, color analysis, algorithm application, and rendering layers.

AI / Algorithm Layer

K-Means clustering for dominant color segmentation. Daltonization coefficient matrices per CVD type applied in CIE Lab perceptual color space for Protanopia, Deuteranopia, and Tritanopia.

Clinical Validation

Evaluated by 30 end users, 10 IT experts, and 3 clinical specialists from the Philippine Eye Research Institute (PERI). Acceptance threshold exceeded across all evaluated criteria.

Delivery

End-to-end ownership from scoping and clinical protocol design through Google Play Store publication. Managed developer, clinical, and end-user workstreams concurrently under a hard academic deadline.
